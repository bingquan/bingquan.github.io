About

Bio

I am a Principal Member of Technical Staff at DSO National Laboratories and an Adjunct Assistant Professor at the National University of Singapore. My research spans AI safety, adversarial machine learning, and the security of agentic AI systems.

My early work focused on assistive robotics — developing intent-recognition and adaptive control algorithms for lower-extremity rehabilitation exoskeletons at NUS. Over time my interests shifted toward the security and reliability of learning-based systems: adversarial examples, backdoor attacks, unlearnable data, and multimodal deception detection.

Currently I am most excited about three interconnected questions: how world models can enable reliable long-horizon planning, how agents can bootstrap and improve their own capabilities autonomously, and what guarantees can be made about safety when LLMs act as autonomous agents in multi-agent pipelines.

Beyond the research itself, the part of the job I enjoy most is mentoring — whether it's NUS students or the interns who pass through my team, watching someone develop their first real research instinct never gets old. Outside work, you'll find me playing tennis or gaming. I'm also in the long, humbling process of learning digital drawing, the piano, and how to juggle.

Research

Current Interests

🌍

World Models for Long-Term Planning

Structured latent-space representations that let agents plan over extended horizons without ground-truth environment rollouts — stable training, low compute, high-speed planning.

LeWorldModel: Stable End-to-End JEPA from Pixels Helios: Real-Time Long Video Generation
🔄

Self-Improving Agents

Systems that autonomously refine their own capabilities — from optimising evaluation harnesses end-to-end to evolving reusable skill libraries through text-space edits.

Meta-Harness: End-to-End Optimisation of Model Harnesses SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills
🛡️

Safety of Agentic Systems

Understanding safety failures that emerge when LLMs act autonomously — from single-agent red-teaming to indirect prompt injection in multi-agent pipelines.

DTap: Controllable Red-Teaming Platform for AI Agents OMNI-LEAK: Multi-Agent Data Leakage via Indirect Prompt Injection
